The Deer Woman in My Mind

Wearing flowers on her head, the deer woman in my mind,
She's starving but never dead, the only one of her kind.
Next to the gleaming rainbow stream,
She plays her flute for the sad, low trees.
Listening to what they all feel and think,
Collecting tears of joy for her to drink.
Her world is dimly lit by the candle in the sky,
Her poor, rugged tent is where she will reside.
In my life I always find, the deer woman in my mind.
